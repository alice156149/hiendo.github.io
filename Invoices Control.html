Invoices Control

Invoices Tracking

Invoice tracking helps streamline the approval process, prevent budget overruns, avoids duplicate payments, enhances financial reporting and decision-making, improve efficiency, and reduce errors.Below are some issues that usually encounter in invoice processing and the propose solutions to tackle those.

Issues with invoices control

Incorrect or Incomplete Documentation
  • Missing purchase orders or delivery receipts
  • Inaccurate descriptions of work or materials and different in unit rate
Discrepancies
  • Mismatches between invoice amounts and contract terms
  • Differences between billed quantities and actual quantities received or used
Late Submission
  • Delayed invoices from subcontractors or suppliers
  • Late submission impacting cash flow and project timelines
Approval Delays
  • Slow internal approval processes
  • Multiple layers of approval required
Duplicate Invoices
  • Accidental submission of the same invoice multiple times
  • Lack of a robust system to track and prevent duplicates
Budget Overruns
  • Invoices exceeding the allocated budget
  • Unapproved change orders leading to unexpected costs

Proposed solutions

Establish Clear Procedures
  • Develop standardized processes for invoice submission, review, and approval
  • Train all relevant personnel on these procedures to ensure consistency
  • Review and update procedures regularly to adapt to project needs and feedback
Centralized Invoice Submission
  • Require all invoices to be submitted through the invoice management system
  • Ensure that invoices are complete and accurately documented before submission
Streamlined Approval Workflow
  • Define clear roles and responsibilities for invoice approval
  • Use automated notifications and reminders to speed up the approval process
Continuous Training and Improvement
  • Offer regular training sessions for staff on the new system and procedures
  • Collect feedback and continuously improve the invoice management process
